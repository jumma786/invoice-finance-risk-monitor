How to read this

Exposure is net ledger (gross invoicing − credit notes) advanced at an 80% rate. Dilution is credit notes as a share of gross — the classic invoice-finance loss signal. Concentration is the largest single stock-line as a share of a client's ledger. Dormancy tiers: slowing >30d, dormant >90d. Priority = risk score × funding-in-use, so the watchlist ranks by money actually at risk, not raw signal — a maxed-out score on a £0-exposure account is a note, not an action.

Two early-warning models. The scorecard is a transparent weighted blend — dilution 50%, concentration 25%, dormancy 25% — a documented rule with no trained label and no leakage. The predictive run-off model above is validated out-of-time against the ledger's own forward outcomes, and beats the scorecard, which stays as its explainable baseline.
