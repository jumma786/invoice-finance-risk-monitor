How to read this

Exposure is net ledger (gross invoicing − credit notes) advanced at an 80% rate. Dilution is credit notes as a share of gross — the classic invoice-finance loss signal. Concentration is the largest single stock-line as a share of a client's ledger. Dormancy tiers: slowing >30d, dormant >90d.

Risk scorecard. The score is a transparent weighted blend — dilution 50%, concentration 25%, dormancy 25%, each normalised to 0–1. It's a documented rule (no trained label, no leakage), exactly how credit scorecards work.
